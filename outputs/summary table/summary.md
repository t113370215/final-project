Phase 1: Baseline Comparison (Looking Only at Gender)
Overview: In this initial stage, we looked at the big picture to see if there is a general weight difference between guys and girls, without considering any exercise habits.

Conclusion: Gender makes a huge difference on its own. On average, guys naturally have a significantly higher baseline body weight compared to girls.


Phase 2: In-Depth Breakdown (Bringing in the Exercise Factor)
Overview: In this advanced stage, we added exercise habits into the mix to look closely at how working out affects guys and girls separately.

Within-Male Comparison: Having an exercise habit makes a massive difference for guys (F = 183.1492, p < 0.001). Guys who exercise regularly weigh 4.31 kg less on average than those who don't.

Within-Female Comparison: Working out also matters a lot for girls (F = 63.5041$, p < 0.001). Girls who exercise regularly weigh 2.65 kg less on average than those who don't.

Side-by-Side Comparison: While exercising successfully helps both genders stay lighter, the actual weight drop and impact are much stronger for guys than for girls (4.31\text{ kg} > 2.65\text{ kg}). Because exercise has a more powerful effect on guys, it proves that gender and exercise habits work together to influence weight differently.
